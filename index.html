About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Psychology at the University of Bologna, specializing in social and political psychology. My research examines how intergroup relations, prejudice, discrimination, and collective action shape social change.

Across my academic and applied work, I use psychological theory and quantitative methods to study how people understand group differences, respond to inequality, and engage with social issues. I am especially interested in connecting rigorous research with practical insight for education, counseling, and applied behavioral contexts.

Academic Research

My academic work is grounded in social psychological theory, empirical research, and quantitative analysis. I study intergroup relations, prejudice, stereotyping, discrimination, tolerance, dehumanization, inter-minority contact, and engagement in social change.

Psychological Counseling

I offer online, psychology-informed counseling-oriented sessions for individuals seeking clarity, support, and practical strategies for navigating personal, academic, and professional challenges.

My work is especially relevant for people dealing with stress, adjustment, uncertainty, life transitions, academic pressure, career-related concerns, and questions of identity or belonging.

I also work with immigrants, expats, and international students navigating life abroad, cultural transition, loneliness, adaptation, and the emotional complexity of moving between countries, communities, and education systems.

To inquire about an online session, please reach out via email. (I aim to respond to booking inquiries within 8 hours.)

Possible Session Themes

Stress & Adjustment Academic Challenges Career Transitions Self-Understanding Psychoeducation Personal Development Resilience Immigrant & Expat Adjustment International Student Wellbeing Cultural Transitions
Please note: These sessions are educational and supportive in nature and are offered within the scope of my training and applicable regulations. They are not a substitute for emergency care, psychiatric treatment, formal diagnosis, or licensed psychotherapy. If you are in crisis or need urgent mental health support, please contact local emergency services or a licensed mental health professional in your area.

Educational Services

I provide educational consulting for international students who are exploring degree programs abroad. Drawing on my experience in international student recruitment, higher education, and cross-cultural academic pathways, I help students make informed decisions about studying internationally.

This service is designed for students from around the world, including but not limited to students from Türkiye, who are considering undergraduate, master’s, or doctoral study abroad.

How I Can Help

  • Program exploration: identifying degree programs, universities, and countries that fit the student’s goals, background, and budget.
  • Application strategy: building a realistic application list and timeline for international admissions.
  • Statement and CV guidance: helping students structure personal statements, motivation letters, academic CVs, and application materials.
  • Academic pathway planning: discussing undergraduate, master’s, and Ph.D. routes, including transitions between education systems.
  • Cross-cultural preparation: helping students understand expectations, adaptation challenges, and academic life abroad.
